What is exclusive OR?
The bitwise exclusive OR of integers

(let us call it

) is defined as follows:
When

is written in base two, the digit in the

's place (

) is
1

c_1, c_2, ...c_m
whose binary representations have
1
in the
2^k
's place, is odd, and
0
if that count is even.
For example, the exclusive OR of
3
and
5
is
6
. (When written in base two: the exclusive OR of
011
and
101
is
110
.)
